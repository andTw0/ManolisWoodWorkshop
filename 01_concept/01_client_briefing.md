# 01 — Client Briefing

## Purpose of this file

This file is the **truth-context base** for the client situation. Use it whenever the project starts drifting or when a design/technical decision needs to be justified.

The project is not a generic webshop. It is a staged development concept for a real small client: **Manolis Tsouris**, an olive wood artist from Crete with strong offline tourist interest but no functional online structure.

---

# 1. Client summary

## Client

**Manolis Tsouris**  
Olive wood artist / local workshop owner in Crete.

## Business type

Handmade olive wood art and objects:

- sculptures
- masks / faces
- bowls
- kitchen objects
- jewelry / small pieces
- wall pieces
- strange one-of-a-kind objects
- tourist-memory pieces

## Current situation

Manolis has:

- a real physical shop/workshop in Crete
- many photos on Facebook
- strong tourist interest
- a domain: `eloslocalart.com`
- no reliable structured website
- no organized online product inventory
- no fixed ecommerce setup
- no online payment process yet
- limited budget
- language barrier: Greek + basic English

## Client wish

The client wants people to:

- find him online
- see his work
- somehow order or ask about products
- experience a website that feels **crazy and alive**, not like a standard shop

---

# 2. Core project problem

The main problem is **not** simply “he needs a webshop.”

The real problem:

> Tourists love the physical shop and want to buy later from abroad, but there is no structured online place where they can rediscover the work, contact Manolis, and ask about available pieces.

---

# 3. Core opportunity

The opportunity:

> Turn offline tourist memories into online inquiries and future orders.

This means the first digital version should focus on:

- recognition
- trust
- personality
- gallery browsing
- easy contact
- product inquiry

Not full automatic ecommerce yet.

---

# 4. Main concept

## Working concept name

**When Madness Met Wood**

## Secondary slogan

**Don’t buy souvenirs. Buy originals.**

## Project sentence

> A dark, warm, living online workshop for Manolis Tsouris — showing his olive wood originals, his personality, and giving tourists a simple way to ask about pieces after they return home.

---

# 5. Brand personality

## The website should feel like

- handmade
- rustic
- warm
- slightly chaotic
- personal
- eccentric
- Cretan
- full of wood and stories
- alive
- memorable

## The website should not feel like

- sterile ecommerce
- generic Shopify template
- luxury minimal gallery
- corporate portfolio

# 6. Research observations from provided images

The shop images show:

- dark interior walls and black display boxes
- warm spotlights
- dense hanging wooden objects
- sculptures and masks outside the shop
- handmade signage
- rough concrete floors
- chaotic but charming display style

---

# 7. MVP definition

## Stage 1 — Website / Gallery

The first deliverable should include:

- homepage with personality
- product/work gallery
- about/Manolis story section
- visit/contact page
- WhatsApp/email/phone contact
- address/map area
- EN/DE language support or structure

## Stage 2 — Catalogue / inquiry shop

The second deliverable should include:

- product categories
- product cards
- product detail view
- inquiry button per product
- request similar piece option
- no fixed price requirement
- no real checkout yet

## Not included in first MVP

- full cart
- live Stripe/PayPal checkout
- automatic inventory management
- complex CMS handover
- shipping calculation
- legal webshop terms

These belong to a later roadmap phase.

---

# 8. Main strategic decision

## Chosen MVP strategy

**Inquiry-based catalogue first. Full checkout later.**

## Why

Because the client currently has:

- no structured inventory
- no fixed product catalogue
- no online payment setup
- negotiated/variable pricing
- handmade unique products
- limited technical ability

A real checkout would be fake or unstable at this stage.

The correct first version is:

```text
See piece → ask about piece → discuss availability/shipping personally
```

---

# 9. Success criteria

The first version is successful if a visitor can:

- immediately understand the vibe of the workshop
- recognize the real shop from photos
- understand that pieces are handmade originals
- browse categories/products
- ask about a piece via WhatsApp/email
- find the physical location
- understand the site on mobile
