# 02 — Target Audience

## Purpose of this file

This file defines who the website is for, what they need, and how the site should guide them.

Use this file to stop the project from becoming a generic product shop.

---

# 1. Primary audience

## International tourists who already visited the workshop

These are the most important users.

They have already seen the physical shop, met Manolis, or remember the objects. They may have taken photos, followed the Facebook page, or talked about ordering later from home.

## Their situation

They are probably:

- back home after visiting Crete
- remembering the workshop
- looking for a specific piece or similar object
- unsure how to contact Manolis
- unsure if shipping is possible
- maybe not comfortable calling internationally

## What they need

- recognition: “yes, this is the place”
- clear gallery of work
- simple way to ask about an item
- WhatsApp/email contact
- trust that the shop is real
- reassurance that pieces are handmade and unique
- shipping inquiry path

## Main user flow

```text
Tourist remembers shop
→ searches Manolis / Local Art Workshop
→ lands on website
→ recognizes photos and slogan
→ browses Originals / Gallery
→ opens a piece
→ clicks “Ask about this piece”
→ sends WhatsApp/email inquiry
```

---

# 2. Secondary audience

## Tourists planning a Crete trip

These users have not visited yet, but they may discover the workshop while planning a route.

## Their situation

They are probably:

- planning a holiday in Crete
- searching for unusual stops
- interested in handmade local art
- looking for something more memorable than standard tourist shops
- using mobile while traveling

## What they need

- strong first impression
- location/address/map
- visit reason
- opening/contact info if available
- photos of the physical shop
- TripAdvisor/social proof
- easy navigation on phone

## Main user flow

```text
Tourist plans route in Crete
→ finds website
→ sees shop photos and reviews/social proof
→ checks location
→ saves/contact/visits workshop
```

---

# 3. Tertiary audience

## Handmade craft / gift buyers

These users may not know Manolis from a trip, but they are interested in original handmade pieces.

## Their situation

They may be:

- searching for original wood art
- looking for unique gifts
- interested in Mediterranean/olive wood objects
- less emotionally connected to the physical shop

## What they need

- strong product imagery
- clear handmade story
- categories
- contact/inquiry path
- shipping possibility
- trust signals

---

# 4. Audience priorities

The website should prioritize audiences in this order:

```text
1. Tourists who already visited
2. Tourists planning to visit
3. General handmade/gift buyers
```

This matters because the site should not behave like a cold online store. It should behave like a memory trigger and contact bridge.

---

# 5. Emotional needs

The user is not only buying an object.

They are buying:

- a memory of Crete
- a story
- a handmade original
- a connection to the artist
- a piece that feels found, not mass-produced

This is why the site language should use words like:

- original
- handmade
- one-of-a-kind
- workshop
- Crete
- olive wood
- ask
- discover
- visit

Avoid overusing words like:

- cart
- checkout
- stock
- SKU
- discount
- sale
- bestseller

---

# 6. Device behavior

The requirements state that the website must work especially well on mobile because tourists may browse while traveling.

Therefore, mobile UX is critical.

## Mobile priorities

- fast loading
- readable text
- large buttons
- simple navigation
- visible WhatsApp CTA
- easy map/contact access
- gallery that does not feel broken

---

# 7. Language needs

The MVP requires EN/DE structure.

## English

Primary international language. Should be simple and readable because Manolis has limited English and visitors come from many countries.

## German

Important because many tourists in Crete are German-speaking and the assignment requires EN/DE.

## Future languages

Potential later languages:

- Greek
- French
- Italian

Do not build these in the first prototype unless there is extra time.

---

# 8. Tone for users

## Good tone

```text
Warm
simple
personal
slightly weird
direct
handmade
human
```

## Bad tone

```text
corporate
luxury-generic
overwritten
fake poetic
too clean
too salesy
```

```text
When madness met wood.

Handmade olive wood originals from Crete.
Step inside Manolis’ workshop and ask about unique pieces shipped from the island.
```

```text
Each piece is handmade and unique. Availability, price, and shipping are confirmed personally.
```

```text
Saw something you like? Ask Manolis about this piece or request something similar.
```
